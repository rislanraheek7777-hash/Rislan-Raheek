Supercopa de España History

The Supercopa de España (Spanish Super Cup) is Spain's annual super cup competition. It originally featured the winners of La Liga and the Copa del Rey. Since 2020, it has been played as a four-team mini-tournament involving the league and cup winners and runners-up. Real Madrid has won the competition 13 times, making them the second most successful club after Barcelona.


First Supercopa Title (1988)

Background

Real Madrid entered as La Liga champions and faced Barcelona, the Copa del Rey winners.

What Happened?

  • Won the first leg 2–0 at the Santiago Bernabéu.
  • Goals came from Míchel and Hugo Sánchez.
  • Lost the second leg 2–1 at Camp Nou.
  • Won 3–2 on aggregate.

Importance

This was Real Madrid's first-ever Spanish Super Cup and the beginning of their success in the competition.


Three Consecutive Titles (1988–1990)

1989

Real Madrid won both La Liga and the Copa del Rey in the previous season.

Because they completed the domestic double, the Super Cup was awarded automatically without a final being played.

1990

  • Opponent: Barcelona
  • Aggregate score: 5–1

The team dominated their biggest rivals and secured a third consecutive Supercopa title.


1993 – Defeating Cruyff's Barcelona

Why It Was Special

Barcelona's famous "Dream Team" under Johan Cruyff was dominating Spanish football.

Final

  • First leg: Real Madrid 3–1 Barcelona
  • Second leg: Barcelona 1–1 Real Madrid
  • Aggregate: 4–2

Heroes

  • Iván Zamorano
  • Alfonso Pérez

Madrid successfully ended Barcelona's challenge and lifted their fourth Supercopa.


Galácticos Era (2001–2003)

2001

Real Madrid defeated Zaragoza 4–1 on aggregate.

2003

The Galácticos team featuring:

  • Zinedine Zidane
  • Luis Figo
  • Raúl González
  • Roberto Carlos

won the Supercopa by defeating Mallorca 4–2 on aggregate.


2008 – Dramatic Comeback Against Valencia

Final

  • Lost the first leg 3–2.
  • Won the second leg 4–2 away from home.
  • Aggregate victory: 6–5.

Despite playing with ten men during part of the second leg, Madrid produced one of the greatest comebacks in Supercopa history.



Mourinho vs Guardiola (2012)

One of the Greatest Supercopa Finals

The rivalry between:

  • José Mourinho
  • Pep Guardiola

reached its peak.

Final

  • Aggregate score: 4–4.
  • Real Madrid won on away goals.

Key Player

Cristiano Ronaldo scored a famous goal at Camp Nou and celebrated in front of Barcelona supporters.

The victory signaled Madrid's growing strength during the Mourinho era.


Cristiano Ronaldo Golden Era (2017)

Final

Real Madrid defeated Barcelona 5–1 on aggregate.

Highlights

  • Cristiano Ronaldo scored at Camp Nou.
  • Marco Asensio scored spectacular goals.
  • Madrid looked unstoppable after winning the Champions League.

This team is considered one of the strongest in club football history.


New Four-Team Format (2020)

In 2020, the competition changed from a two-team final to a four-team tournament.

Road to the Title

  • Beat Valencia in the semifinal.
  • Drew with Atlético Madrid in the final.
  • Won on penalties.

Importance

It was the first Supercopa won under the new format and one of the first trophies of the second era of coach Zinedine Zidane.


2022 – Ancelotti's First Supercopa

Route to Glory

  • Beat Barcelona 3–2 in the semifinal.
  • Defeated Athletic Club 2–0 in the final.

Key Players

  • Luka Modrić
  • Karim Benzema

This title helped launch Madrid's memorable 2021–22 season, which ended with La Liga and Champions League triumphs.


2024 – Thirteenth Supercopa

Final

Real Madrid faced Barcelona in Saudi Arabia.

Match

  • Real Madrid won 4–1.
  • Vinícius Júnior scored a hat-trick.
  • Madrid dominated from start to finish.

Legacy

The victory brought Real Madrid's total to 13 Spanish Super Cups, further cementing their place among Spain's greatest teams.


Final Statistics

  • Title: 13
  • Runner-up finishes: 8
  • First Title: 1988
  • Latest Title: 2024
  • Most Frequent Rival in Finals: Barcelona

Real Madrid's Supercopa history is filled with Clásico triumphs, Galáctico stars, Cristiano Ronaldo's brilliance, and modern dominance, making it one of the club's most celebrated domestic competitions.
